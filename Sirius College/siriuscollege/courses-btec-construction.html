Construction

Length of course

1 year.

Entry requirements

Students should be working towards GCSE grades of C and above.

What will I learn?

This is an opportunity to work towards a Level 2 qualification where you will learn both the theory and practical side of the construction industry. The course covers a wide range of areas within construction and is designed for students who may wish to work or study further in construction but have not yet picked an area of interest.

Within this subject you will study:

The construction industry as a whole, where you will look at job roles and responsibilities, the different types of clients and projects within construction and the social and economic aspects of the industry

Health & Safety, where you will explore the safety of those working on site, risk assessments, COSHH assessments and analyse accident statistics

Sustainability, considering how building projects can be designed to benefit users and the environment in both the long and short term

Building technology, finding out how the different components of a building are put together to ensure a successful project

Carpentry & joinery, which would involve using various hand and power tools to look at a range of jointing of timber techniques

Trowel operations, where you will explore the laying of bricks and blocks using tools and different bonding arrangements - this involves working outdoors

Painting & decorating, using different types of paint to achieve a variety of quality finishes and exploring other finishes such as wallpapering

Construction drawing, which involves using manual drafting techniques to produce a series of architectural drawings Building services installation, the basics of plumbing and wiring component installation
